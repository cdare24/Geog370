In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Changing projections in QGIS is actually very easy. Using the data given by world projections you click the button at the right bottom corner which has a sphere icon with a cone over it (similar to how a conic projection was demonstrated as being made. From there you are able to search for different projection models.

WGS84 Projection

This projection distorts both the size and the shape of countries as they move to be further from the equator. You can see this distortion because of the Indicatrix Mapper pluggin. The circles aroun the equator show that section of the map is it's true size and shape while the further you get from the equator the circles grow bigger and form more of an eclipse shape.

Aitoff Projection

This projection distorts the size and shape of countries as they move away from both the equator and the prime meridian. This means that as you get closer to the north and south pole the map becomes bigger and as you move away from the Prime Meridian you also get bigger. The areas of the map that are most distorted are the corners.
